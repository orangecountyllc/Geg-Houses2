Business Services under the Business Services Agreement

Services of Service Company. Subject in each case to provisions of Sections 3 and 4 and the other terms and conditions of this Agreement, applicable law and PC’s control and direction of the Practice, Service Company will provide, arrange for, or advise or assist PC with, the following services with respect to the business aspects of the Practice, directly or through subcontractors of Service Company:

a) General Business and Administrative Services. Service Company shall provide, arrange for, or advise and assist PC with, business and administrative services reasonably required for the operations of the Practice, including the following:

(i) Providing or arranging for dental and office inventory, supplies and equipment required for operation of the Practice;

(ii) Arranging for the provision of all utilities, telephone service, Internet service, janitorial services, repairs, laundry, linen, uniforms, printing, stationery, forms, postage, duplication services and transcription services required for operation of the Practice;

(iii) Providing or arranging for clerical, secretarial, reception and other administrative, support services and other non-clinical personnel required for operation of the Practice (including personnel who may also provide services for other persons);

(iv) Recruiting, on behalf and subject to the control of PC, Dental Personnel (as defined below); provided that, for the avoidance of doubt, decisions with respect to the hiring, engagement, supervision, salary, benefits, retention and termination of any and all Dental Personnel shall be controlled by PC and not by Service Company;

(v) Providing or arranging for financial systems, including bookkeeping, accounting, patient billing and collection systems for the Practice;

(vi) Providing or arranging for a patient appointment scheduling system, provided that, for the avoidance of doubt, the hours of operation, scheduling of patients and assignment of patients to specific Dental Personnel or other staff shall be controlled by PC;

(vii) Advice and consultation with respect to agreements for the benefit of PC with third party payors, including health maintenance organizations, preferred provider organizations, Medicaid, employer organizations and other plans for the provision of dental services and the payment therefor, to persons enrolled in such plans, subject to PC’s control of decisions regarding fees and pricing;

(viii) Assistance with or arranging for payment of accounts payable of PC to the extent that PC makes funds available therefor;

(ix) Advice and assistance with, and providing or arranging for, marketing, advertising, signage and public relations services in promoting the Practice;

(x) Advice and assistance with, and providing or arranging for the lease, purchase or other provision of, supplies, materials, instruments, furnishings, furniture and equipment required for the operation of the Practice, and the repair and replacement thereof; provided that PC shall have sole care, custody and control of all dental equipment, dental instruments, dental materials and dental supplies;

(xi) Advice and assistance in the storage and organization of files and records, subject to PC’s ownership and control of patient records with respect to the Practice;

(xii) Advice and assistance in PC’s licensing and preparation and filing of annual reports and tax returns required to be filed by PC with respect to the Practice;

(xiii) Advice and assistance in financial planning, analysis and budgeting for the Practice;

(xiv) Advice and assistance in procuring and continuing insurance coverages necessary for operation of the Practice;

(xv) Providing or arranging for payroll and payroll accounting services for the Practice;

(xvi) Advice and assistance in establishing and administering a compliance program for the Practice;

(xvii) Advice and assistance with respect to the lease, build-out and equipping of each office Location; and

(xviii) Advice and assistance with, or providing or arranging for, such other business, administrative and financial consultation and advice as may be required from time to time for the operation of the Practice or mutually agreed upon from time to time by the parties.

b) Billing, Collection and Disbursement Services. Service Company shall advise and assist PC, on behalf and in the name of PC, in billing PC’s patients and third party payors for professional fees and other charges of PC for goods and services rendered in the Practice, and in collecting such fees and charges from PC’s patients and third party payors. Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, Service Company shall provide or arrange for, or advise and assist, PC with the following, and PC appoints Service Company as its true and lawful agent and attorney-in-fact to take the following actions for and on behalf of and in the name of PC. Such appointment shall survive the termination of this Agreement for all accounts receivable arising from billing for goods and services provided prior to such termination, until all obligations of PC owed to Service Company shall have been paid in full.

- Submitting claims and bills to patients of the Practice and their third party payors in the name and on behalf of PC or the Dental Personnel, as applicable;

- Collecting, whether from patients or from third party payors, accounts receivable generated by billings done in the name of PC or any of the Dental Personnel and depositing the same as provided in Section 6;

- Receiving payments from whatever source for goods and services rendered by PC or any of the Dental Personnel in the Practice, including payments from patients, Blue Shield plans, insurance companies, Medicaid and any other third party payor;

- Taking possession of, endorse on PC’s behalf and cause to be deposited any notes, checks, money orders, insurance payments and any other instruments received in payment of such accounts receivable and to deposit same as provided in Section 6;

- To the extent PC provides or makes funds available to Service Company, including through advances (if any) by Service Company, disburse funds in payment of accounts payable or other amounts owed by PC with respect to the Practice or in payment for goods or services on behalf of PC with respect to the Practice, including payment of Service Fees or other amounts payable to Service Company hereunder; and

- Taking such other actions relative to such billing, collection and the financial matters of the Practice as are appropriate and consistent with sound business practice, the purposes of this Agreement and applicable law.

For the avoidance of doubt, Service Company will not control nor have responsibility for the establishment or determination of the amount of professional fees charged by PC or the Dental Personnel.

PC acknowledges and agrees that Service Company cannot guarantee or control the creditworthiness of any patient or third party payor, the exercise of discretionary authority by any payor or the satisfaction of any patient with services rendered and, therefore, Service Company shall not be liable for any failure to collect or receive payments on behalf of PC hereunder, except for any failure arising directly and solely out of Service Company’s gross negligence or willful misconduct. To facilitate Service Company’s collections of PC’s accounts receivable, upon request PC shall assist and cooperate with Service Company in its billing and collection efforts.
