About

Transactions Director, Equity Group (since 2020)

 

Prior Business Experience

Paul Foster is a seasoned professional with over 25 years experience advising various businesses on mergers, acquisitions and divestitures of companies or businesses ranging in value from under $5 million to over $2 billion on five continents.

During that period, led over 80 acquisitions and 20 divestitures to completion.


Transaction Advisory Group, LLC ( 3/2019 - Present)

Provide independent M&A advisory services to businesses and management teams who don’t require or can’t justify hiring and investment banking firm. Primary focus is on structuring and negotiating transactions.

  • Advising two private equity firms in evaluating prospective portfolio additions and working with existing portfolio companies in completing additional “Roll-Up” acquisitions.

  • Advised senior management team of a small broker/dealer and developed a negotiating strategy during the acquisition of their firm.

  • Advising management team of private software company on their proposed sale.


    • Managing Director, Global Corporate Development - The Bank Of New York Mellon (“BNY”) (7/2007- 3/2019)

    Responsible for oversight, (direct and/or advisory) of all facets of the merger, acquisition and divestiture activities within the Investment Services businesses of the company, which contribute approximately $12 billion in revenue and $4.4 billion in operating profit annually.

    This includes the identification and evaluation of potential acquisition or alliance targets; associated financial modeling and analysis; structuring and pricing of transactions assuring that they meet minimum corporate selection criteria; development and presentation of Term Sheets, and direct negotiation of the transaction's terms and related legal documentation with the current owners or their representatives.
    Act as a strategic and tactical advisor to senior management in evaluating and completing a transaction.

    Accomplishments include:

  • Completed approximately ~15 acquisitions which have largely accounted for the segment’s revenue growth in recent years as well as 15 divestitures, which rationalized redundant or non-core operations resulting from the merger of The Bank of New York and Mellon and other prior acquisitions.

  • Evaluated various strategic corporate initiatives, and where appropriate developed various programs to facilitate their implementation.

  • Led several business major transformation projects, restructuring underperforming businesses and products to increase profitability.



    • Senior Vice President, Corporate Development - The Bank Of New York (“BNY”) (5/1995 - 6/2007)

    Responsible for all facets of BNY’s M&A and divestiture activities (as detailed above) focusing on the Investment/Wealth management, Execution Services (B/D and Clearing) and Corporate Trust businesses.

    This required the development and maintenance of constant lines of communications with potential acquirees and their representatives, including attorneys and investment bankers to articulate the Bank's competencies and strategies in order to generate enthusiasm for the potential partnership, while also gaining an understanding of the goals and intentions of the other parties for use in developing negotiating strategies.

    Accomplishments include:

  • Closed over ~100 transactions (of several thousand evaluated and analyzed) including 11 investment and wealth management acquisitions; 15 separate acquisitions of B/Ds and related technology firms which created BNY Securities Group; several securities clearing acquisitions including Pershing, as well as >30 corporate trust roll-up transactions.

  • Completed several major strategic divestitures including the LBO/spin off of BNY Securities Group into ConvergEx, and the outright sales of BNY Financial Corp (factoring) , BNY’s credit card business, mortgage servicing business, and non-strategic branch locations.

  • Directed several strategic initiatives including the initial implementation of a major reengineering and cost reduction program which included the direct management of the consultants involved.



    • Vice President, Corporate Strategy and Planning - The Bank Of New York (“BNY”) (5/1989 - 4/1995)

    Responsible for annual corporate plans and development and implementation of various corporate strategies. In addition, participated in/led BNY’s M&A transactions , largely focusing bank/branch acquisitions (prior to formation of Corporate Development Department).


    Vice President, Asset Liability Management & Organizational Profitability Measurement - Manufacturers Hanover Trust Company - (JP Morgan Chase)


    Developed performance measurement and evaluation criteria at a business unit level for all areas of the corporation and supported the corporate Asset Liability Management Committee with up- to-date funding performance and gap analyses.
